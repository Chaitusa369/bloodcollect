Blood donation


Blood usage in World


Blood contains many life-saving components that can help to treat different illnesses and injuries. For many patients, blood donors are their lifeline.

Blood goes to those having medical emergencies such as accident survivors; individuals undergoing major surgeries such as organ transplants; patients with cancers and blood disorders like leukaemia and lymphomas or severe anaemia; and even new-borns with medical conditions.


How much blood is needed


Every hour of the day, 15 units of blood are used. We need about 120,000 units of blood to meet the transfusion needs of patients every year, equivalent to about 400 units of blood a day.

With an ageing population, more advanced life-saving medical procedures, and new hospitals being established, more blood will be needed every year.

Therefore, to ensure a steady supply of blood for public use in peacetime and during emergencies, it is vital that a 9-day supply of blood is maintained in our national inventory.

Selective blood donations


From time to time,the national blood donor recruiter, may also make appeals for blood donors of specific blood groups or rare blood groups to meet the medical needs of our patients.

Such appeals will be for specific blood groups that are currently in short supply, such as O+ or A-, or they might be needed by patients with rare blood groups. As group O blood may be used in a patient having severe trauma or other bleeding emergency whose blood group has not been determined, it is important that the Bloodbank@bloodcollect holds higher levels of O+ and O- blood stocks.

In times of need, suitable donors might be contacted via phone, SMS or mail.

National Blood Programme


About the programme


The key objective of Indiaâ€™s National Blood Programme is to ensure that all patients in India, regardless of nationality and residential status, have access to a safe and sufficient blood supply when needed.

In order to ensure a healthy and constant supply of blood at all times, blood collect strives to enlarge the donor pool in India by recruiting and retaining donors who are residents and non-residents alike, based on the voluntary non-remunerated blood donation principle.

This approach of altruistic donation and inclusive recruitment ensures that the transfusion needs of all patients in India are adequately and safely met.

Transfusion medicine


Overview

The Blood Services Group (BSG) provides advisory, patient management, training and research services in clinical transfusion medicine.

The BSG Laboratory also offers a wide range of laboratory services in blood transfusion medicine, such as:


  • Blood processing and inventory
  • Blood group serology
  • Blood donor testing
  • In-process control
  • Immunohaematology Laboratory
  • Tissue typing
  • Cell processing laboratory and research
